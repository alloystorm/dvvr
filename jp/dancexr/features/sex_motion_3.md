## Comprehensive Overview

The system is designed to manage the complex physical interactions between characters, covering both general setup and specific details for different body areas.

### 1. Body Part Control
This section allows granular control over individual body parts (e.g., head, arms, torso, legs).
*   **Key Function:** Defines how different parts move relative to each other.
*   **Setup:** Requires defining physical constraints and behavior for specific parts.

### 2. Behavior State
This manages the overall behavioral state of the characters.
*   **Purpose:** Dictates the character's current emotional/emotional state.
*   **Details:** Involves managing the overall mood.

### 3. Body Area Control
This module provides high-level control over broad areas of the body.
*   **Examples:** Focuses on head, torso, and limbs.
*   **Functionality:** Used for broad gestures and posture control.

### 4. Interaction Flow
This manages the sequence of physical interactions.
*   **Role:** Dictates the sequence and transitions between different physical states.
*   **Logic:** Handles state management.

### 5. Physical Relationship
This is crucial for defining *how* characters interact physically.
*   **Concept:** Defines mutual physical constraints (e.g., sitting together, standing together).
*   **Control:** Manages the overall physical framework of the interaction.
